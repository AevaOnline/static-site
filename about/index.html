I'm incurably geeky and incorrigably inquisitive, which may be why I'm also a chronically poor sleeper. I am politically queer because there is no other ethical choice, and I'm queer just because that's who I am. I grew up stretched between several cultures - Dutch Catholic, and Jewish, and Hindi - and after twenty years studying the Buddha Dharma, I carry on practicing my own mix of it all. I'm also a survivor of a long-term disability, and I'm trans.

I've decided to talk openly about being transgender, both online and offline, and sometimes even in front of large audiences, because I think it's important to raise awareness around issues that would have saved me a lot of suffering growing up. Being trans, coming out as trans, especially mid-career, is also a complicated business, and an intensely personal one. Don't assume that anything I say about my journey is true for any other person of trans experience.

I came out in 2017, in the beginning of the Trump administration, and I ended up jobless and uninsured for a year because of it. While I'm thankful for the chance to reflect on and re-contextualize my life, I'm even more grateful to all the friends who have supported me along this journey. ðŸ’œ
