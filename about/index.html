What I do ...

I'm the Open Source Program Manager for the Azure Confidential Computing team, for which I work with and track events in a few different Open Source Foundations (CCC, CNCF, OIF, OSI) on behalf of Microsoft.

You might have heard of a few projects I've worked on previously: mysql, openstack, ironic, and kubernetes.

I'm also active in a number of my local (non-tech) communities, was recently published in an Anthology of Trans Buddhist authors, and am working on my own book now.

Why I do it ...

I'm incurably geeky and incorrigably inquisitive, which may be why I'm also a chronically poor sleeper. I am politically queer because there isn't any other ethical choice, and I'm simply queer because that's who I am. I practice my own admixture of the buddha dharma, hindu, jewish, and wiccan traditions which I grew up with. I believe in the oxford comma. I'm also a survivor of a long-term disability (I spent half of my 20's home-bound with lyme disease) and trans gender.

I've decided to talk openly about being trans, both on here and on other social media, and offline (sometimes in front of large audiences). Publicly transitioning is a complicated aspect of each person's story; don't assume anything I say translates to any other person of trans experience. When I transitioned mid-career, I found myself jobless and uninsured for about a year -- during the Trump administration! This taught me a lot about privilege in a way that was very difficult, but in hindsight I'm also very grateful for.

I'm thankful for the chance to reflect on and re-contextualize my life, and to all the friends who supported me through this journey. ðŸ’œ
