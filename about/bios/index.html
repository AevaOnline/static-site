Bios and Headshots

Short and Personal (500 characters)

Aeva Black is an incurably queer geek, a Seattle'ite, and an open source hacker in the Azure Office of the CTO, passionate about making our digital world more secure, ethical, and inclusive. At the moment, their efforts are focused on open source software supply chain security, cloud native computing, and confidential computing. In a past life, they led the creation of OpenStack's Bare Metal Cloud project, lived on a tiny farm in the Olympic Mountains, and raised a pair of wolves.

General Purpose (1,100 characters)

Aeva Black is an open source hacker in the Azure Office of the CTO, working on software supply chain security, confidential computing, and cloud native computing. They serve on the Board of the Open Source Initiative, the non-profit which stewards the definition of "open source", and previously held positions in the Confidential Computing Consortium and on the OpenStack Technical Committee. While a Master Engineer at HP Cloud in 2013, Aeva led the creation of the OpenStack "Ironic" bare metal cloud project, which continues to power important non-profit scientific workloads such as CERN.

Aeva is a queer and non-binary geek, a lifelong student of the buddha dharma, and an aspiring author who was recently published in Transcending, an anthology of trans buddhist authors. In addition to their technical work, they are committed to improving diversity and inclusion in the tech industry, advocating for digital privacy rights and consent culture. They also served for several years on the Board of the Consent Academy, a Seattle-area non-profit that advocates for consent in all aspects of life.

Open Source - Full Length (1,500 characters)

Aeva Black is a dot-com veteran, an open source hacker, a policy wonk, and a queer and non-binary geek. Their career started in '99 in the media and gaming industry, building decentralized network services with open source software, and has included leading roles in both startups and in big tech. From their current position in the Azure Office of the CTO's Open Source Ecosystems team, Aeva advises many teams working on software supply chain security, confidential computing, and digital privacy.

Aeva brings to policy and governance discussions a perspective that is grounded in years of engineering leadership and which centers impacts on under-represented groups. They founded the OpenStack Ironic project in 2013 while working at HPE.

Aeva's prior community work includes supporting the growth of the Confidential Computing Consortium, hosting meetups and unconferences, and serving on the OpenStack Technical Committee and the Kubernetes Code of Conduct Committee. They brought a framework for Consent Culture to Kubernetes, and instated a restorative approach to incident handling based on years of volunteering with local communities in the Pacific Northwest.

Aeva also served on the Board of Directors for the Consent Academy, a Seattle-area educational non-profit, for several years. They were published in 2019 in "Transcending", an anthology of trans buddhist writers, and are the technical editor of a forth-coming book, "Trust in Computer Systems and the Cloud" (eta Jan '22).
