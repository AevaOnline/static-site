Slightly Longer (1,000 characters)

Aeva Black is a dot-com veteran who is now dedicated to making open source technology more secure, ethical, and inclusive. They work in the Azure Office of the CTO with a focus on open source software supply chain security and community safety, and serve as a community-elected member of the Board of the Open Source Initiative and an appointed shadow to Board of the Cloud Native Computing Foundation. Aeva previously held positions in the Confidential Computing Consortium, the OpenStack Foundation, and the Consent Academy. Aeva also founded the OpenStack Bare Metal Cloud project, and, many years ago, managed a few small MySQL databases.

Their recent writing includes a contribution to Transending: An Anthology Of Trans Buddhist Voices (2019), and acting as the technical editor for Trust In Computer Systems And The Cloud (2021). A frequent speaker at tech conferences, Aeva's unique perspectives often inspire others to question their assumptions.

Aeva Black is a dot-com veteran, an open source hacker, a policy wonk, and a queer and non-binary geek. Their career started in '99 in the media and gaming industry, building decentralized network services with open source software, and has included leading roles in both startups and in big tech. From their current position in the Azure Office of the CTO's Open Source Ecosystems team, Aeva advises many teams working on software supply chain security, confidential computing, and digital privacy.

Aeva brings to policy and governance discussions a perspective that is grounded in years of engineering leadership and which centers impacts on under-represented groups. They founded the OpenStack Ironic project in 2013 while working at HPE.

Aeva's prior community work includes supporting the growth of the Confidential Computing Consortium, hosting meetups and unconferences, and serving on the OpenStack Technical Committee and the Kubernetes Code of Conduct Committee. They brought a framework for Consent Culture to Kubernetes, and instated a restorative approach to incident handling based on years of volunteering with local communities in the Pacific Northwest.

Aeva also served on the Board of Directors for the Consent Academy, a Seattle-area educational non-profit, for several years. They were published in 2019 in "Transcending", an anthology of trans buddhist writers, and are the technical editor of a forth-coming book, "Trust in Computer Systems and the Cloud" (eta Jan '22).
